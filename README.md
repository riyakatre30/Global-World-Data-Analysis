##  Project Title

**Global World Data Analysis using Python**

##  Purpose

The purpose of this project is to analyze global country-level data and discover
meaningful insights related to population, economy, healthcare, environment, taxation,
employment, and land resources. The project uses Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and data 
visualization techniques to compare countries and identify important global trends.

##  Business Problem

Governments, researchers, and organizations need meaningful insights from global data 
to make informed decisions regarding economic development, healthcare planning, environmental 
sustainability, and resource management. Raw data alone is difficult to interpret, so this project 
transforms country-level data into clear visualizations and actionable insights.

##  Tools & Technologies Used

* Python
* Jupyter Notebook
* Pandas
* NumPy
* Matplotlib
* Seaborn

## Features

* Data Cleaning and Preprocessing
* Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
* Country-wise comparison
* Top 10 country analysis
* Correlation analysis using Heatmap
* Bar Charts, Line Charts, Pie Charts and Heatmaps
* Identification of global trends and patterns
* Data visualization for better understanding

##  Key Insights

The analysis revealed several important global trends across different indicators. **China** has 
the highest population, the largest urban population, and the highest CO₂ emissions, reflecting its 
large population size and strong industrial development. **Russia** has the largest land area, while **Monaco** 
has the highest population density due to its small geographical area and concentrated population. **India** stands 
out with one of the largest armed forces and a high percentage of agricultural land, highlighting its significance in both
defense and agriculture. **Niger** records the highest birth rate, indicating rapid population growth, whereas **Cuba** has 
the highest number of physicians per thousand people, demonstrating better healthcare availability. The analysis also shows 
that **Qatar** has the highest labor force participation rate, while **South Africa** experiences the highest unemployment 
rate, indicating major differences in employment conditions across countries. In terms of taxation, **Comoros** has the 
highest total tax rate, whereas **Algeria** records the highest tax revenue in the dataset. Furthermore, the comparison 
between GDP and tax revenue indicates that countries with stronger economies generally collect higher tax revenue, although 
taxation policies vary from country to country. Overall, the project highlights significant differences among countries
in terms of population, economy, healthcare, agriculture, employment, taxation, and environmental impact, demonstrating 
how data visualization can transform raw data into meaningful insights for better understanding and decision-making.
